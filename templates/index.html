📖 What Do These Numbers Mean?
⚠️
Why does Acc±200 look low (~33%)? Don't worry, that's actually normal!

Acc±200 counts a prediction as "correct" only if it's within ±200 cycles of the real answer. Since bearing life can span 0 to 2000+ cycles, that's a really tight target — like throwing darts at a moving bullseye. On these benchmark datasets, even the best models typically hit 30–55% at this level. So 33–34% is right on track. Check Acc±500, R², and MAE for the bigger picture.

Metric Meaning Good range
MAE Average prediction error (in cycles) — lower is better < 300 cyc
RMSE Like MAE but punishes big misses more harshly < 450 cyc
R² How much of the pattern the model "gets" (1.0 = perfect) > 0.70
MAPE Percentage error relative to actual values < 40%
Band What it counts Typical score
Acc±50 Very strict tolerance 10–20%
Acc±100 Strict tolerance 20–35%
Acc±200 Moderate tolerance ← yours 30–55%
Acc±500 Practical maintenance window 60–85%
💡 Good to know: What matters most for real-world maintenance is catching failures in the danger zone (RUL < 200 cycles).
